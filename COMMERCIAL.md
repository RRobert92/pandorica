# Commercial Licensing

`pandorica` is released under the
[PolyForm Noncommercial License 1.0.0](LICENSE). That license is free for:

- academic and nonprofit research, regardless of funding source
  (NIH/NSF/ERC/Wellcome/HHMI grants count as noncommercial under PolyForm);
- charitable, educational, public-research, public-safety, public-health,
  environmental, and government institutions;
- personal study, hobby projects, and other noncommercial work.

**Anything else needs a separate commercial license.** Examples that require
one:

- use inside a for-profit company (including internal R&D and pipelines that
  feed a commercial product or service);
- contract research organizations (CROs) and other for-profit service
  providers, even when the end client is academic;
- offering `pandorica` (modified or not) as part of a paid product,
  cloud/SaaS service, or commercial workflow;
- bundling, redistributing, or sublicensing `pandorica` under different
  terms.

If you are not sure which category you fall into, ask — the contributor would
rather answer a quick email than have you guess.

## How to obtain a commercial license

Email **robert.kiewisz@gmail.com** with the subject line
`Pandorica commercial license — <your company>` and the following:

1. **Company / organization.** Legal name, country of registration, website.
2. **Contact.** Name, role, email of the person handling the license, plus
   the technical contact if different.
3. **Intended use.** What you plan to do with `pandorica` — in-house data
   processing, integration into a product, redistribution, hosted service,
   etc. A few sentences is fine.
4. **Scope.** Approximate number of users / seats / nodes, deployment context
   (workstation, on-prem cluster, public cloud, customer-facing service).
5. **Modifications.** Whether you plan to modify the code, and whether you
   intend to redistribute those modifications (as source, as binary, or
   embedded in another product).
6. **Timeline.** When you want to start, and whether you need a perpetual,
   annual, or evaluation-term license.

A reply is typically sent within **5 business days**. After that, the
licensor will send back a short commercial license agreement tailored to the
scope and a quote; the agreement is signed by both parties before any
commercial use begins.

## Evaluation

`pandorica` does not include a built-in commercial evaluation period.
If you need to evaluate it inside a for-profit organization before
committing to a license, ask for a **short-term evaluation license** in the
email above (usually 30 days, no fee). State the evaluation's goal and
scope so the licensor can scope the agreement appropriately.

## Why a commercial-license layer

The project is intended to be free for science and paid for by industry, so
that academic users never pay and commercial revenue funds maintenance.
Releasing under PolyForm Noncommercial makes that split unambiguous and
enforceable — without it, the project could not sustainably stay free for
the academic community it serves.
